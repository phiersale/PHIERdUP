Step One — Fix the Biggest Bill

Everything starts with healthcare.

Right now millions of families are one medical bill away from disaster. Routine care is buried under premiums, deductibles, and surprise bills.

When healthcare is unstable, everything built on top of it is unstable too — jobs, households, small businesses, even local governments.

PHIERS fixes the foundation.

When routine care drops from about $10,000 to about $600, the pressure finally lifts.

Families stop choosing between:

  • medicine and groceries
  • doctor visits and rent
  • care and bankruptcy

Workers can change jobs without risking their family's coverage. Small businesses can hire without drowning in insurance costs. Unions can fight for wages and working conditions instead of spending every contract battle defending healthcare.

That's where stability begins.

🎥 The Math Behind the Movement (3:33)
$10,000 to $600. One conversion funds 12 more. This is how we win.

Step Two — Let Paychecks Work Again

When healthcare stops swallowing every raise, something important happens:

People finally feel their paychecks again. Raises actually raise living standards.

Workers gain leverage because they're no longer chained to their employer for insurance. Businesses compete for workers again. Money starts circulating in local communities instead of disappearing into insurance headquarters.

That's when the middle of the economy starts getting stronger again.

📈 The Cascade Math
One person switches. That funds 12 more. Those 12 fund 148. No new taxes. Just math.

Step Three — Put the Money Back Where It Belongs

Right now about $2.7 trillion a year flows through the healthcare system. A huge chunk of it disappears into paperwork, middlemen, and waste.

PHIERS doesn't create new money. It just stops lighting existing money on fire.

When that waste disappears, the savings go back into the people and communities that created them.

That means investment in things people actually need:

  • housing people can afford
  • schools that work
  • reliable infrastructure
  • food security
  • communities that can stand on their feet again

The money doesn't disappear. It just stops being siphoned off.

Step Four — The Country Stabilizes

When the biggest pressure on family budgets finally drops, the country starts to feel different.

Lower costs. Stronger communities. More stability.

Life stops feeling like a constant emergency.

And when life gets easier, something bigger happens:

People start trusting the country again. Not because someone gives a speech — but because their lives actually improve.
