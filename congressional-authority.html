The Affordable Care Act Gives Congress Full Authority

The ACA explicitly gives Congress the power to expand covered healthcare benefits.

What This Means

Congress can authorize telehealth as a covered benefit through a simple legislative amendment to the ACA.

No constitutional amendment needed.

No Supreme Court approval required.

No state-by-state implementation.

They can do this tomorrow with a majority vote.

How Authorization Works

Congress passes a bill that says:

"Telehealth services from accredited providers shall be covered as an essential health benefit under the Affordable Care Act at a cost of $600 per beneficiary per year, funded through Medicare/Medicaid programs and available through ACA marketplace enrollment."

That's it. One legislative amendment. Universal coverage authorized.

The CMS Connection

CMS (Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services) already manages 120 million beneficiaries.

Once Congress authorizes telehealth:

  • CMS immediately begins enrollment for Medicare/Medicaid beneficiaries
  • Private insurers face competition (must lower prices or lose customers)
  • Gig workers/uninsured can enroll through ACA marketplaces
  • Cascade savings begin within 30 days

Timeline from authorization to universal coverage: 8-13 months

If Congress can do this tomorrow, why don't they?

Insurance company lobbying: $371 million spent in 2024

Campaign donations: Millions to both parties

Revolving door: Former Congressional staffers now work for insurers

Congress protects insurance profits, not American lives.
