PHIERS is a not-for-profit cooperative — built by people who refused to stop, owned and operated by its members, accountable to We the People.

Three components make it work:

PHIERSale — Our cooperative marketplace uses collective bargaining and group discounts on health-related goods and services. When members purchase medications through PHIERSale, we generate over $50 per month in savings per member. That $50 sponsors a Medicaid beneficiary into existing telehealth — saving CMS at least $9,400 per year. Those savings sponsor more people. The cycle builds until coverage reaches everyone.

PHIERWorks — Job training and placement that strengthens and expands the safety net while providing all workers with real benefits — creating good jobs in the communities that need them most: rural areas, urban centers, and everywhere in between.

PHIERPlace — Resource matching and referral connecting people to the care, services, and support they need, wherever they are.

Together these three components create a safety net for the safety net — universal coverage, paid for entirely by the savings the system itself generates.
No new taxes. No new costs. Just redirecting money already being wasted into a solution that already exists.

This is not another insurance plan. This is the path to government-sponsored universal healthcare — built from the ground up, by people serving people, for, by, and accountable to We the People.

What is the Survey?

The PHIERS survey lets constituents communicate their priorities directly to elected officials. When 1,500 people in a district complete the survey, their representative is summoned to a town hall where they must publicly address those demands and commit to acting on them.

Candidates seeking office must commit to those demands before November 2026 — publicly, on the record.

This is direct democracy in action.

How Does Replacement Work?

When an elected official refuses to respond to constituent demands, PHIERS.org organizes the district to pursue every available constitutional tool — primary challenges, special elections, and organized constituent campaigns — to replace them with someone who will do the job.

We do not wait for the next scheduled election to apply pressure.

Organized constituents with documented demands are the most powerful force in any district. That power begins the moment you sign.
