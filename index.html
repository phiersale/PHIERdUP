Healthcare is collapsing. The money is already there.

We spend over $10,000 per person per year on “healthcare” in the U.S. Most of it never reaches actual care. It disappears into admin, billing games, and insurance overhead — while people skip basic treatment, drown in debt, or die waiting.

The leverage Congress won’t talk about:

With existing ACA authority and modern telehealth, we can deliver a basic care floor for about $600 per person per year.

That’s enough to cover primary care, mental health, and chronic care management for roughly 80% of Americans — without new taxes and without killing private insurance.

The problem isn’t that we don’t have the money. The problem is that Congress refuses to re-aim it. PHIERS is the plan to force that re-aiming — or replace the people blocking it.

Why this can actually work

  • 3.5% rule: History shows that when ~3.5% of people actively engage, systems change.
  • District-level pressure: Members of Congress don’t fear “the nation.” They fear their own district.
  • Replacement threat: When enough voters in a district coordinate around a single demand, incumbents move — or lose.
  • Existing law: We’re not waiting for a miracle bill. We’re using authority that already exists.
Our target:

Reach the 3.5% engagement threshold in enough districts to force a national realignment on healthcare by July 4, 2026 — or send a wave of replacements to Congress who will finish the job.

We are not asking you to “believe” in a fantasy. We are asking you to help apply the minimum force required to make Congress do the job they already owe us.
