Our Healthcare System Is Dangerously Vulnerable

Supply Chain Reality

90% of U.S. medications are imported — mostly from countries that could cut us off at any time.

Single points of failure: When one factory in China or India shuts down, Americans can't get life-saving medications.

Price manipulation: Foreign suppliers can charge whatever they want because we have no domestic alternatives.

This isn't just about cost — it's about national security. A healthcare system that depends on foreign supply chains for 90% of its medications is not a healthcare system. It's a vulnerability waiting to be exploited.
