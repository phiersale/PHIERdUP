Why the System Keeps Failing

Here's how government usually works: they try to fix things one at a time. A healthcare bill here, a jobs bill there. But the problems don't hit one at a time — they hit all at once.

And monied interests control public policy. Lobbyists, insurance companies, and pharmaceutical corporations spend billions to keep Congress focused on their priorities — not yours. The result is a system that protects profits while regular people pay the price.

  • Medical debt weakens households.
  • Job instability weakens communities.
  • Weaker communities shrink tax revenue.
  • Shrinking revenue strains public services.

Move one piece of the puzzle — the others shift.

You can't fix a connected problem with disconnected solutions.

What This Costs Regular People

  • Small businesses hesitate to hire.
  • Unions spend bargaining power defending benefits instead of raising wages.
  • Families delay care until problems get worse.

And when work gets shaky, everything else starts slipping too.

When work becomes unstable:

  • Housing insecurity rises.
  • Local economies weaken.
  • Public services get stretched thin.

The pressure stacks up.

The Opportunity Hidden in Plain Sight

If we can fix healthcare, we get leverage over Congress to fix everything else.

Solving healthcare first generates the savings needed to fund the other solutions. Roughly $2.7 trillion a year — freed from a bloated system — gets reinvested in wages, infrastructure, public safety, and a more inclusive economy.

When PHIERS delivers universal telehealth coverage, union members will no longer need employers to carry those crushing benefit costs. That frees up money for higher wages, more hiring, better work-life balance — without costing employers a dime more. We get manufacturing, infrastructure, a stronger safety net, and a healthier workforce.

Solve healthcare and you give people real leverage over Congress. That leverage is how we force them to end the unauthorized war. That leverage is how we demand better decisions on every issue that matters.
