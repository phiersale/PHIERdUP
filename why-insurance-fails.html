Insurance Profits Require Denial of Care

Insurance companies don't make money by paying claims. They make money by collecting premiums and denying claims.

The Math of Insurance Profits

Revenue: $10,000 premium per person √ó 100M people = $1 trillion

Claims paid: $700 billion (70%)

Profit: $150 billion (15%)

To increase profit, they must deny more claims or raise premiums.

This isn't an accident. This is the business model.

The Denial Machine

  • "Not medically necessary" ‚Äî Even when doctor orders it
  • "Out of network" ‚Äî Artificial provider restrictions
  • "Pre-authorization required" ‚Äî Delays that turn into denials

Administrative Waste

15-20% of every healthcare dollar goes to insurance administration:

What That Money Funds

  • Claims processors who deny care
  • Billing departments that fight denials
  • Lawyers who defend denials
  • Call centers that frustrate patients
  • Executives who make $20M+ salaries

None of this provides healthcare. It's all overhead protecting profits.

The Network Scam

Insurance companies restrict which doctors you can see. Why?

Control and profit.

  • They negotiate lower rates with "in-network" doctors
  • They charge you 2-3x more for "out-of-network" care
  • They make switching providers difficult
  • They limit specialist access

The result: You pay for insurance but can't see the doctor you need.
