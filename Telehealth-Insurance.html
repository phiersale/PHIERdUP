Telehealth vs Insurance

The System Is Rigged.
The Fix Is Simple.

Most Americans think healthcare and insurance are the same thing. They're not. And that confusion is costing you $10,000 a year.

✍ Sign the Petition → See the Math
The Problem

You're Paying for
Protection You Never Use.

Insurance was only ever designed for rare, catastrophic events — hospitalizations, surgeries, emergencies, specialty care.

Meanwhile, 80% of what people actually need is basic, everyday care — primary care, urgent care, mental health, chronic care, preventive care, prescriptions, and labs.

Insurance plans often block access, delay treatment, or refuse to pay for these simple needs.

80%
of insured Americans never use hospitalization or specialty care
$10,000
what they pay per year for protection they don't need
$600
what telehealth costs — covering everything they actually use

There's only one logical solution: care and catastrophe protection must be separated. PHIERS does exactly that.

The 80/20 Model

Telehealth Is Care.
Insurance Is Protection.

The 80/20 Healthcare Model: Telehealth = Everyday Care, Insurance = Catastrophic Protection

Telehealth — $600/yr

The 80% you actually use:

  • ✓ Primary care
  • ✓ Urgent care
  • ✓ Mental health
  • ✓ Chronic disease management
  • ✓ Prescription refills
  • ✓ Preventive care
  • ✓ Basic labs

No claims. No networks. No middlemen. No prior authorization. Just care.

Insurance — Backstop Only

The 20% you rarely need:

  • → Hospitalizations
  • → Surgeries
  • → Emergency trauma
  • → Long-term rehab
  • → Expensive imaging
  • → Catastrophic events

Rare — but extremely costly. Insurance is the backstop, not the front door.

Telehealth delivers 80% of healthcare at a fraction of the cost — roughly 1/14th.

The PHIERS Savings Cascade

When Telehealth Handles the 80%,
Everything Gets Cheaper.

  • ER visits drop
  • Hospital readmissions drop
  • Insurance claims drop
  • Premiums drop
  • Medicaid costs drop
  • Medicare costs drop
  • ACA subsidies drop
  • Private insurance stabilizes

Telehealth shrinks the cost of the entire system. Insurance protects against the worst events.

Together, they create a system that is:

Affordable — 80% cost reduction + cascading savings

Scalable — Works for 234 million Americans

Defensible — Market-based, not government-mandated

Universal — Accessible to everyone

The Key Insight

Telehealth is care.
Insurance is protection.
PHIERS combines them.

Once you separate care from catastrophe, the math becomes simple — and universal coverage becomes possible. Congress could authorize this tomorrow. They just haven't had to.
