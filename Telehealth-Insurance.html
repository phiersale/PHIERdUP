The Problem Congress Won't Name

Most Americans believe healthcare and insurance are the same thing.

They're not.

Insurance was designed for rare, catastrophic events — hospitalizations, surgeries, emergencies. But somewhere along the way, it became the front door to all care. That's why costs exploded.

80% of what people actually need is basic, everyday care — primary care, urgent care, mental health, chronic disease management, prescription refills, preventive care, and labs.

Insurance companies block access, delay treatment, and deny claims for these simple, routine needs — while charging thousands of dollars a year for the privilege.

The front door costs $10,000/year. Most people only needed a $600 door all along.

What Telehealth Actually Does

Telehealth isn't insurance — it's direct care. No middlemen. No prior authorization. No billing codes. No networks. No claims.

Just care.

Telehealth covers the services people need constantly:

  • Primary care
  • Urgent care
  • Mental health & counseling
  • Chronic disease management
  • Prescription refills
  • Preventive care
  • Basic labs
  • Care navigation

Telehealth delivers 80% of healthcare at roughly 1/14th the cost of insurance.

Available 24/7. No waiting rooms. No referrals. No network restrictions.

The cost difference is staggering — and it's why the insurance industry lobbies to keep telehealth off the ACA Exchange.

The 80/20 Model: Care and Catastrophe — Separated

Insurance was never meant to be the front door. It was built to protect against catastrophic events that are rare but ruinously expensive:

  • Hospitalizations
  • Surgeries
  • Emergency trauma
  • Long-term rehab
  • Expensive imaging

These events are rare. But extremely costly when they happen.

Insurance is the backstop — not the front door.

PHIERS separates the two:

📱 Telehealth — The 80%

Everyday care. $600/yr. Direct access. No gatekeepers.

🏥 Insurance — The 20%

Catastrophic backstop. Lower cost. Reserved for real emergencies.

The 80/20 model: telehealth covers everyday care, insurance protects against catastrophe. Together — universal, affordable coverage.
