SIMPLE MATH

The Numbers Behind Stabilization

▶ Watch: The Simple Math — $600 vs $10,000 and the Cascade

1. Current Cost — $10,000

$10,000

The average person in America pays about $10,000 a year for healthcare.

That includes premiums, deductibles, co-pays, what employers pay, and everything else stacked on top. For a lot of people, it's even more than that. For most families, this is the biggest bill after housing.

For most families, that's rent money.

2. Telehealth Cost — $600

$600

A full year of telehealth coverage costs about $600 per person.

Telehealth covers routine care, prescriptions, preventive care, and basic diagnostics. Serious or specialized care still happens in person. Telehealth just handles the everyday stuff that makes up most care.

That's a phone bill. Not a mortgage payment.

▶ Watch: How PHIERS Aligns with Trump and Ami Bera's Cost Goals

3. The Difference

$10,000 – $600 = $9,400

Switching from $10,000 to $600 is a 94% drop in routine healthcare cost.

The math isn't complicated. It's straight subtraction.

That's money that stays in your pocket instead of going to an insurance company.

4. The Expansion Model

$9,400

That $9,400 can cover about 12 more people.

One person switching helps twelve more get covered. Lower cost = more people covered. That's the whole idea.

One person opting in helps a dozen more get covered. That's not a theory. That's just division.

5. System Impact

When healthcare costs drop, everything else gets easier:

Workers can move jobs without fear

Small businesses save money

Unions gain bargaining power

Emergency spending goes down

Families can finally breathe

And the savings from this model help fund the other four dimensions of the PHIERS plan.

6. The Conclusion

This model doesn't need new taxes. It works because the cost goes down.

That's not politics. That's just arithmetic.

When costs fall, people breathe easier. It's that simple.
